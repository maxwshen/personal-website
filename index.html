Max W. Shen

My research uses applied machine learning and statistical methods for fundamental scientific discovery and high-impact applications. Some interests include genome editing, directed evolution, and the intersection between deep learning, causal inference, and Bayesian models. As a computationalist at heart, I believe in interdisciplinarity, getting one's feet wet, and that better solutions to real-world problems arise by marrying each problem's unique structure with thoughtful modeling and inference design.
During my Ph.D. at MIT, I have published co-first author papers in Nature and Cell. Currently, I am completing my Ph.D. and am actively looking for opportunities for late 2020 or early 2021.
I performed my Ph.D. work with David R. Liu and Aviv Regev at the Broad Institute from 2018-now. Before, I started my Ph.D in Computational & Systems Biology with David Gifford in the MIT Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence Laboratory from 2016-2018 while collaborating closely with Richard I. Sherwood. Before that, I graduated summa cum laude with a B.S. in Computer Science with a specialization in bioinformatics from U.C. San Diego and did research with Pavel A. Pevzner and Mingxiong Huang.
